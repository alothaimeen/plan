Exercise 1: Reading - The Unification of Saudi Arabia

King Abdulaziz, a gifted leader, was responsible for the legendary event that marked the beginning of modern Saudi Arabia. In 1902, he regained Riyadh, beat the city garrison, took the Masmak Fortress, and established his headquarters in the city. He expanded Saudi Arabia to include all of the Hijaz, Makkah and Madinah and united all the tribes.

On September 23, 1932, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia was officially established, with Arabic as the national language and the Holy Qur'an as its constitution. King Abdulaziz initiated the building of infrastructure and set Saudi Arabia on the road to modernization.

Answer the following questions:

1 When did King Abdulaziz regain Riyadh?
2 What did King Abdulaziz take in Riyadh?
3 When was the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia officially established?
4 What are the two main things that became official in Saudi Arabia?
5 What does "set Saudi Arabia on the road to modernization" mean?

Exercise 3: Reading - The Space Race

The Russians launched the world's first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, on October 4, 1957. The Americans won the race to land on the moon more than ten years later—with Apollo 11 on July 20, 1969.

"This is one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind," said astronaut Neil Armstrong, the first person to walk on the moon.

His Royal Highness Prince Sultan bin Salman Al Saud flew on STS-51G Discovery in June 1985 as the representative of ARABSAT. He was the youngest person to fly on the Space Shuttle at the age of 28.
